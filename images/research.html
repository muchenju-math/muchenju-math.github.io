Research

My research interests lie primarily in probability and combinatorics, with an emphasis on random graphs, random matrices, and statistical inference on networks. Current topics of interest include:

  • Opinion-exchange dynamics on random networks
  • Detection and recovery of planted structures in random graphs
  • Spectral properties of random matrices

I have also worked on problems in Ramsey theory, additive combinatorics, and Schubert calculus.

As an early-career researcher, I aim to explore a broad range of areas in mathematics. If you would like to discuss mathematics or collaborate on a problem, please feel free to stop by my office or send me an email.
